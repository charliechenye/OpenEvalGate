# Code of Conduct

OpenEvalGate is intended to support rigorous, practical discussion about production AI quality, governance, and launch decisions. Contributors are expected to help maintain a professional and evidence-oriented community.

## Expected behavior

- Treat other participants with respect.
- Critique ideas, designs, evidence, and code rather than people.
- State assumptions and uncertainty clearly.
- Welcome contributors with different technical, product, policy, and domain backgrounds.
- Keep examples free of private customer data, credentials, and confidential company information.
- Give appropriate credit when reusing another contributor's research, templates, examples, or code.
- Accept maintainers' decisions about project scope and documented non-goals.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, discrimination, or personal attacks.
- Publishing another person's private information without permission.
- Deliberately submitting proprietary, confidential, or unsafe material.
- Misrepresenting test results, evidence, authorship, or project endorsement.
- Repeatedly disrupting discussions after a maintainer has made a scope or moderation decision.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove comments, issues, pull requests, or contributions that violate this policy. They may also temporarily or permanently restrict participation when necessary to protect contributors and the project.

For sensitive conduct concerns, contact the maintainer through [chenyezhu.com](https://chenyezhu.com/) rather than opening a public issue. Reports will be handled as privately as reasonably possible.

## Scope

This policy applies to repository discussions, issues, pull requests, reviews, and other spaces where someone is representing OpenEvalGate.
